About us

We revive lost Ukrainian heritage through 3D reconstruction

Our mission is to digitally restore cultural monuments destroyed or lost over time,
so future generations can see and feel their beauty

Who we are

We combine science, creativity, and modern technology to preserve the past for the future. Since 2020, students and lecturers of the Department of Geometric Modeling at NTU “KhPI” have been working on creating 3D reconstructions of Ukraine’s historical and cultural landmarks.

Our projects bring to life unique architectural monuments in digital form — the Shydlovskyi Estate, the Hryhorii Skovoroda Museum, a traditional Ukrainian cottage, the Bekariukov Manor, the Chervonohorod Castle, and many others. On this website, you can explore the stages of reconstruction, watch video presentations, and view completed D models created by our students.

We also develop custom tools for 3D reconstruction — Python-based software modules that enhance the processes of modeling, analysis, and visualization. The 3D reconstruction direction at our department continues to grow, welcoming new ideas, participants, and partners.

If you are passionate about history, architecture, or digital technologies, you can apply to join the project or leave us a message through the feedback form. Together, we are creating Ukraine’s digital heritage — so that future generations can see, study, and feel it.

How we work

Our work is grounded in research, accuracy, and a deep respect for cultural heritage. Every reconstruction begins with collecting historical photographs, archival materials, and textual descriptions, especially when the original structure has been partially or completely lost. By combining photogrammetry, manual modeling, and modern visualization tools, we recreate architectural objects that can no longer be documented by traditional methods.

This approach not only preserves valuable historical information but also makes it accessible to educators, students, researchers, tourists, and anyone interested in Ukraine’s cultural legacy. Our interactive 3D models serve educational, scientific, and social purposes — offering virtual access to heritage sites for people who may never have the opportunity to visit them in person.
