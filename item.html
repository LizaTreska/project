Case

National Literary and Memorial Museum
of Hryhorii Skovoroda

History

The museum, founded in 1922, was dedicated to the life and philosophy of Hryhorii Skovoroda — one of Ukraine’s most influential thinkers, poets, and musicians. For a century, it preserved rare manuscripts, personal belongings, and exhibits connected to his life. Located in the philosopher’s former estate, it became a quiet cultural center surrounded by nature and memory.

“The National Literary and Memorial Museum of Hryhorii Skovoroda is more than just a building — it’s a symbol of Ukrainian culture and resilience. When I learned it was destroyed in 2022, I wanted to bring it back, at least digitally, so that people could still experience its beauty and spirit.”

Istomina K.A.

Consequences of war

In May 2022, during the full-scale Russian invasion, the museum
was hit by a missile and burned almost completely. Only the outer walls and the statue of Skovoroda survived, standing as symbols of endurance and faith in the future restoration of Ukrainian culture.

“Through 3D modeling in Blender and custom Python add-ons, I
recreated the museum’s architecture, lighting, and surroundings with
realistic detail. This project is my way of preserving cultural memory —
transforming loss into a digital space where history can live on.”

Maistryk S.L.

3D modeling

This 3D reconstruction project aims to digitally restore the museum as it looked before the destruction — preserving its historical appearance and atmosphere through detailed modeling and texture mapping. Using Blender and custom Python add-ons, the reconstruction features realistic lighting, natural surroundings generated with fractal algorithms, and a camera animation following a smooth spiral trajectory.
